Description

Can agentic coding solve the data problem in robotics? Historically, data from physics simulations has been severely limited in its diversity and realism, in part because authoring new simulation environments required extensive manual work from trained experts. As long as every gap between simulation and real requires involvement from both tech artists and robotics engineers, the scalability of simulation data is severely limited.

In the past few months, newfound capabilities of agentic coding are being applied to simulation authoring with impressive results (see invited speakers), holding a promise to alleviate the bottleneck of manual simulation tuning and usher a new era of scalable generation of synthetic robotics data. Agentic coding may allow us to automate every step of the simulation authoring pipeline: create a digital approximation of any robotics scene (and infinite variations thereof), design tasks (rewards and curricula), and reduce sim/real gaps (e.g., tune physics and appearance parameters). At the same time, we don't yet know what are the blockers and limitations, nor do we know where the greatest opportunities are.

The aim of this workshop is to bring together practitioners of this nascent field for knowledge sharing and joint exploration of best practices. Our hope is to establish a roadmap for this nascent approach and gain clarity on current and future blockers and opportunities, as seen from the lens of robotics.

Proposed Format and Interactivity

In practice, agentic authoring relies on harness design: agentic workflows, prompts, multi-agent assemblies, involving different approaches to grounding the simulation with real-world data. Therefore, presenters will be invited to run live interactive sessions where we inspect together the intermediate artifacts and scrutinize how the agent assembles and debugs the simulation environment, engaging all participants in the spectator sport of autonomous problem solving!

To promote interactivity, we will introduce several micro-awards, and invite the attendees to vote for "most creative solution", "best use of limited resources", or similar titles.

04

Contributed Papers Plan

In order to encourage open-source practices that accelerate the entire community, we will require submissions of demos with open source code (as well as a short paper clarifying the novelty of the approach and the relation to other works). Evaluating the submissions will consider the merits of the code: its documentation, ease of use and extension, as well as the clarity of the presentation.

Submission portal & deadlines: coming soon
05

Plans to Encourage Participation and Diversity

Our organizing team and invited speakers represent a diverse range of geographical areas, institutions, and areas of expertise. This will allow us to reach multiple audiences when advertising this workshop. In particular, we will advertise the workshop through channels of the robotics simulation community (e.g., MuJoCo, with its more than 13K followers). In order to further showcase the workshop in a compelling format, we will assemble a compilation of the demo videos of the invited speakers.

This is a new and wide-open corner of robotics that relies on open-source tools and data, and therefore has opportunities for impact even without an expensive robot lab nor large-scale training infrastructure. Therefore, we expect submissions to this workshop will come from a variety of industries, backgrounds, and career stages; we intend to further promote this diversity through the selection process.
